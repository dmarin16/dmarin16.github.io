### Introduction 

Mental health discussion has expanded significantly in the last decade, accelerated most notably by the COVID-19 pandemic. As Nealon (2021) argues, the pandemic functioned as a “mental health accelerant”, increasing public attention to psychological well-being while simultaneously improving access to support resources through digital counselling and community-based interventions. Universities worldwide responded by adapting their mental health initiatives to support students better. For example, the University of Guelph established a Mental Health and Well-Being Task Force in 2023 to co-produce recommendations to strengthen institutional support and advance a “health-promoting university” model. These changes show that mental health and well-being are central to academic success, campus belonging, and sustainable university environments. However, when the discussion centres on the experiences of international students, it tends to focus primarily on statistical terms rather than on real-life experiences. 

### Methodology
In order to go beyond the numbers this project uses a qualitative approach that focuses on the voices of international students instead of a more traditional quantitative approach. The research was guided by two main qualitative methods: Narrative Inquiry and Autoethnography. Narrative inquiry guided the collection and analysis of Reddit stories, while autoethnography guided the collection and analysis of my stories.

#### Narrative Inquiry 
Narrative inquiry is a research method that focuses on understanding people's experiences through their own stories. It uses those stories as data and there are many ways they can be collected. Butina (2015) explains that “Examples of inquiries that yield narrative data include interviews that solicit stories or oral histories, or written autobiographies and biographies” (pp 190) This way we can get a deeper understanding of how people are affected by certain events in their lives. The next step after data collection is analysing the data. Narrative analysis is an important step of narrative inquiry. Thematic analysis is one of the most used approaches when analysing narrative data. Ahmed et al. (2025) explain the six steps thematic analysis uses. They are:
1. Familiarization with the data 
2. Generating initial codes 
3. Searching for themes
4. Reviewing themes
5. Defining and naming themes
6. Writing the report

By using these steps the complexity, depth, and importance of the data can be fully explored and this approach can improve the credibility of the findings.  
 

#### Autoethnography 
Reed-Danahay (2006) explains that autoethnography is a methodology that combines life stories with ethnography allowing the researcher to do both a self-reference and a reference to culture: “A form of self-narrative that places the self within a social context.” (pp 16). Autoethnography has been used to analyse different forms of self-representation that explore the experiences and cultural constructions of people that migrate. It has also been used as a method to avoid the objectification scientific research methods can cause by using personal narratives from both the researcher and a group or community.        
According to Cooper and Lilyea (2022), autoethnography is a qualitative method that allows us to approach a cultural issue through the analysis of personal story. They also explain how we should consider that even though the researcher's story is being analysed does not mean one should rely solely on memory to narrate it. In order to have more detailed data the research can use diaries, notes, photos or any other artifact relevant to the research. Some researchers have used questionnaires to help them through their self-reflection, combining it with a list of the main events arranged in chronological order. 
 
#### Process
The methodologies I used guided how the project was made. The first step was to select certain keywords that would help guide the initial analysis of existing sources. That analysis would be shown in short essays that introduced important themes and made the comparison between quantitative and qualitative methods more evident. The autoethnography part was guided by 6 questions based on each topic. When the essays were completed, many key words appeared. Those keywords were then used to guide the narrative inquiry, data collection, and analysis of Reddit posts. After collecting the data, the stories selected were put in separate documents. Voyant Tools was used to do the text analysis. Voyant showed the most used words, the links between words used, and Voyant created the word cloud and links used for each video essay. Everything was then added to the webpage.
